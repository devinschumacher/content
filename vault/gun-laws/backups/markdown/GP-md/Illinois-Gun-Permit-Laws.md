**Permit Requirements for Purchases:** Individuals must have a FOID to purchase rifles, shotguns, or handguns. A 24-hour waiting period is required for rifles and shotguns, while a 72-hour waiting period is required for handguns. Exceptions apply to law enforcement officers, authorized dealers, or non-residents at approved gun shows.

**Record-Keeping for Sales:** Dealers must keep records of firearm sales for ten years, including the firearm‚Äôs description, serial number, buyer‚Äôs identification information, and FOID number. All purchases are subject to a background check.

**Minimum Age for Firearm Purchases:** The minimum age to purchase a firearm in Illinois is 18. Individuals under 21 need written consent from a parent or guardian, who must also be eligible to obtain a FOID.

**FOID Eligibility Requirements:** Applicants must meet specific criteria, including being over 21, not having felony convictions, not being addicted to narcotics, and not having been a patient in a mental facility in the past five years. The FOID is valid for five years and costs ten dollars.

**Possession and Carrying of Firearms:** Possession of firearms requires a FOID. It is illegal for individuals under 18 to possess firearms. Public possession of firearms is generally unlawful, with exceptions for students engaged in firearm training or approved activities.
