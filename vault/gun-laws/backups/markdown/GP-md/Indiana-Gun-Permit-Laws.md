**Key Points of Indiana Gun Laws:**

**Purchasing Firearms:** Individuals over the age of eighteen are allowed to purchase firearms. Minors can purchase firearms with proper consent from a parent or guardian. It is illegal to sell or purchase a firearm for convicted felons, individuals addicted to drugs or alcohol, or those not mentally proficient to handle a firearm.

**Regulations for Rifles and Shotguns:** Rifles and shotguns are loosely regulated by Indiana law, but specific regulations exist regarding the purchasing and selling of handguns. Handgun transfers require a background check conducted by an authorized dealer at the time of purchase. Law enforcement officials and individuals with a valid concealed carry license are exempt from the instant background check.

**Possession of Firearms:** Indiana licensing is not required for the possession of firearms, whether it be a rifle, shotgun, or handgun. However, regulations exist regarding minors‚Äô possession of firearms in various circumstances.

**Carrying Handguns:** Carrying a handgun requires Indiana licensing, involving a detailed application process and background investigation by law enforcement. Specific eligibility criteria must be met, and the license is subject to renewal after a designated period.

**Specific Provisions:** Certain regulations apply to the possession and transportation of firearms, including restrictions on firearms in school zones and transportation requirements in vehicles.
