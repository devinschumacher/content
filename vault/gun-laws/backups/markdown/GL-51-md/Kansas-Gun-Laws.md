### Firearm Laws in Kansas

Kansas firearm laws prioritize Second Amendment rights while imposing specific regulations:

  * **Registration and Permits:** Kansas does not require firearm registration. A permit is not needed to purchase guns.
  * **Age Restrictions:** Individuals must be 18 to purchase firearms. Minors under 18 may possess firearms under specific circumstances, such as during supervised activities like hunting or competition shooting.
  * **Felony Restrictions:** Felons are prohibited from purchasing or possessing firearms. This prohibition extends to minors convicted of certain offenses.
  * **Carrying Firearms:** No license is needed for shotguns or rifles. A license is required to carry a concealed handgun, and open carrying of handguns is generally restricted.
  * **Eligibility for Firearms License:** Applicants must be at least 21 years old, a Kansas resident for at least six months (or on active military duty), and meet various eligibility criteria including safety training and background checks.



Kansas‚Äô laws aim to prevent illegal firearm use while respecting the rights of responsible gun owners, reflecting ongoing efforts to balance public safety with individual liberties.
