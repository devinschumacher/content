Permit and Registration Requirements

  • Mississippi law does not require a permit to purchase handguns or long guns.
  • Registration and licensing for both forms of firearms are not required.
  • No permit is required to carry a rifle, but carrying can be restricted in certain areas such as schools, places of worship, or government buildings.

Restrictions on Firearm Possession

Certain restrictions pertain to former convicts; it is unlawful for anyone convicted of a felony to possess a firearm unless they have been pardoned or have obtained a rehabilitation certificate.

Concealed Carry Regulations

  • Any person over 18 years of age can carry a concealed firearm within their home or place of business.
  • Concealed carry is also allowed during sporting events that require the use of a firearm.
  • For other instances, a concealed carry license is required.

The application process for a concealed carry permit includes:

  • Applicant must be at least 21 years old.
  • Must have lived in Mississippi for a minimum of 12 months.
  • Must possess a clean criminal background with no history of drug abuse or mental instability.
  • Submission of fingerprints, a full-face photograph, and a fee of approximately $100.
  • Permits are issued 120 days after the request and are valid for four years.

Firearm Dealers Regulations

  • Firearm dealers are subject to more rigorous regulations than buyers.
  • Dealers must keep records of all sales, including the date of sale, description of the firearm, type of caliber, and the name of the purchaser.
  • These records are subject to random police inspection to prevent illegal gun sales.
  • There are no restrictions on ammunition, caliber, or magazine size.
